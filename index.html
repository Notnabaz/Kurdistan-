PÊŞEKÎ Between Empires & Mountains

Kurdistan is not just a geographical region; it is a cultural tapestry woven over millennia. Spanning across the Zagros mountain range, it connects the Middle East, serving as a bridge between civilizations.

Known for its hospitality (*mêvanperwerî*), the region boasts a diverse heritage of language, religion, and art that has survived against all odds.

DÎROK Û ŞARISTANIYET Echoes of the Past

C. 10,000 BC

The Dawn of Agriculture

The region of Kurdistan is part of the Fertile Crescent. Archaeological sites like *Shanidar Cave* provide crucial evidence of Neanderthal culture and early human settlements.

700 BC

The Median Empire

Ancestors of the Kurds, the Medes, established a vast empire. This era is often cited in Kurdish nationalism as the golden age of sovereignty.

"The mountains are our only friends."

MODERN ERA

Resilience & Autonomy

Through the 20th and 21st centuries, Kurds have fought for recognition. Today, the Kurdistan Region of Iraq stands as a beacon of development and relative stability.

ZIMANÊ KURDÎ A Rich Linguistic Tapestry

The Kurdish language belongs to the Indo-European family. It is divided into several main dialects, each with its own literary history and nuance.

Kurmanji (Northern)

Most widely spoken. Written in Latin script.

Sorani (Central)

Dominant in Iraqi Kurdistan. Written in Arabic script.

ÇAND Û HUNER Living Traditions

Newroz

The Kurdish New Year, celebrated on the Spring Equinox with fire jumping and dance.

Govend

Traditional group dances where everyone holds hands, symbolizing unity.

Cil û Berg

Colorful, traditional fabrics tailored uniquely for men (Shal u Shepik) and women.
