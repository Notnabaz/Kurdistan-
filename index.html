Kurdish politics are shaped by regional parties, historical rivalries, and the pursuit of autonomy within neighboring states. Below is a concise, non-exhaustive guide to the main actors and structures.

Kurdistan Democratic Party (KDP)

North / Erbil

One of the largest parties in the Kurdistan Region of Iraq, historically dominant in Erbil and led by the Barzani family. Focuses on security, regional governance, and economic development.

Patriotic Union of Kurdistan (PUK)

Sulaymaniyah

Historically influential in the south of the Kurdistan Region (Sulaymaniyah). Played a central role in modern Kurdish politics and governance; has alternated cooperation and competition with the KDP.

Gorran & Other Movements

Reform Voices

Movements like Gorran (Change) emerged seeking transparency, reforms, and alternatives to long-standing party structures. Civil society and youth movements continue to shape political debate.

Governance

The Kurdistan Regional Government (KRG) administers a degree of autonomy within Iraq, with its own parliament and ministries. Power-sharing between parties is a key feature of the political landscape.

Regional dynamics

Kurdish politics are also influenced by relations with Baghdad, neighboring states, and international partners. Security, oil revenue, and internal reform are ongoing priorities.

Note: This is a short, neutral overview for informational purposes and not a comprehensive political analysis.
